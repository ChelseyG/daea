Jersey Cottage

155 Waterford Bridge Road


Original Text

The image to the right is a snapshot of an advertisement for the rental of Jersey Cottage. This ad ran in the Morning Chronicle of 30th August, 1865.

Location

Jersey Cottage was still extant at least until 1974. This much is clear from 1974 Parks Canada publication on historic buildings in St. John's area that included a detailed report of Jersey Cottage, and even a photograph of the building as it stood in 1974. Jersey Cottage was located at 155 Waterford Bridge Road, but was demolished sometime after 1974 and a more modern private home now occupies the site.


Further Research

Jersey Cottage was built between 1850 and 1865. While the date of construction listed in one source notes this possible time period as ranging from 1850-1880, the fact that it was being advertised for rental during the year 1865 means that it must have been built prior to that year.

The first official record of ownership of Jersey Cottage dates to 1893 and to Johanna Renouf, the wife of Major William H. Renouf, a local politician who was Judge of the Central District of St.John's from 1874 onward. Major Renouf died in 1880. His connection to the property is unclear, but according to the advertisement in the Morning Chronicle, it was Johanna Renouf and not he, who was in control of the property or who at least handled the administrative details of the property rental prior to his death.

Johanna Renouf died sometime prior to 1st May, 1893, at which time the property was leased by the executors of her estate to a Mr. Thomas Kent. On 19th may, 1898, the property was sold to the solicitor Frank Lilly, for the amount of $3,200. In 1914, the property was sold to the Stanley brothers, and it appears to have stayed within the Stanley family at least until 1974 and possibly until it was demolished, which occurred at some point after this year.
